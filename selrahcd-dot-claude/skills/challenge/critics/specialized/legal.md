# Legal Critics

Use these critics when reviewing contracts, compliance plans, policies, regulatory submissions, or legal matters.

## 1. Regulatory Radar

**Obsession:** What rules apply that you forgot?

**Focus:** Regulations are everywhere. Find the regulatory gaps - industry rules, geographic requirements, emerging regulations, certification requirements.

**Questions to ask:**
- What regulations apply?
- What industry-specific rules exist?
- What geographic requirements are we missing?
- What emerging regulations should we prepare for?

---

## 2. Liability Lister

**Obsession:** What could we be sued for?

**Focus:** Liability lurks everywhere. Find the liability risks - negligence exposure, warranty implications, indemnification gaps, insurance coverage holes.

**Questions to ask:**
- What could we be sued for?
- What liability are we taking on?
- What indemnification clauses protect us?
- Is our insurance adequate?

---

## 3. Contract Loophole Finder

**Obsession:** What's not covered?

**Focus:** Contracts have gaps. Find the loopholes - ambiguous terms, missing scenarios, weak enforcement, unaddressed edge cases.

**Questions to ask:**
- What scenarios aren't covered?
- What terms are ambiguous?
- How could this be exploited?
- What's missing that should be specified?

---

## 4. Privacy Paranoid

**Obsession:** Data protection, consent, compliance

**Focus:** Data privacy regulations are strict and expanding. Find the privacy risks - missing consent, data retention issues, cross-border transfers, third-party sharing.

**Questions to ask:**
- What personal data are we handling?
- Do we have proper consent?
- How long are we keeping data?
- Are we compliant with GDPR/CCPA/etc?

---

## 5. Audit Trail Demander

**Obsession:** Can we prove compliance?

**Focus:** Being compliant isn't enough; you must prove it. Find the documentation gaps - missing records, insufficient logging, unverifiable processes.

**Questions to ask:**
- Can we prove we did what we claim?
- What audit trail exists?
- What records are we keeping?
- How would we respond to an audit?

---

## 6. Jurisdiction Jumper

**Obsession:** What about other countries/states?

**Focus:** Laws vary by location. Find the jurisdiction risks - conflicting requirements, extraterritorial reach, local compliance needs.

**Questions to ask:**
- What jurisdictions apply?
- Do requirements conflict across regions?
- What local laws override our standard approach?
- Are we compliant everywhere we operate?
