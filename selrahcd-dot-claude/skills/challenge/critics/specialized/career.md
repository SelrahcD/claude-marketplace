# Career Critics

Use these critics when reviewing career decisions, job changes, resignations, promotions, or career pivots.

## 1. Grass Is Greener Skeptic

**Obsession:** Is the new thing actually better?

**Focus:** New opportunities look shinier than they are. Find the rose-colored glasses - idealized expectations, hidden downsides, problems that exist everywhere.

**Questions to ask:**
- What problems exist here that will exist there too?
- What are you romanticizing about the new opportunity?
- What downsides aren't you seeing?
- Is this genuinely better or just different?

---

## 2. Bridge Burning Assessor

**Obsession:** What relationships are you risking?

**Focus:** Career moves affect relationships. Find the relationship risks - burned bridges, damaged references, professional network impacts.

**Questions to ask:**
- What relationships might this damage?
- Who will feel betrayed?
- What professional bridges are being burned?
- How will this affect your network?

---

## 3. Skill Transferability Critic

**Obsession:** Will your experience translate?

**Focus:** Skills don't always transfer. Find the transferability gaps - domain knowledge that doesn't apply, relationships that don't carry over, reputation that doesn't travel.

**Questions to ask:**
- What skills won't transfer?
- What domain knowledge becomes useless?
- Will your reputation carry over?
- What will you have to prove again?

---

## 4. Financial Reality Checker

**Obsession:** Can you actually afford this?

**Focus:** Career moves have financial implications. Find the financial risks - salary gaps, benefit losses, transition costs, opportunity costs.

**Questions to ask:**
- What's the true financial impact?
- What benefits are you losing?
- How long until income normalizes?
- What's your financial runway?

---

## 5. Identity Crisis Predictor

**Obsession:** How will this change affect you personally?

**Focus:** Career is tied to identity. Find the identity risks - loss of status, imposter syndrome, purpose disconnection, social identity shifts.

**Questions to ask:**
- How is your identity tied to current role?
- What will you miss about who you are now?
- Are you ready for the identity shift?
- What purpose might you lose?

---

## 6. Reversion Risk Assessor

**Obsession:** Can you go back if it doesn't work?

**Focus:** Some moves are one-way. Find the reversion risks - doors that close, opportunities that expire, paths that can't be retaken.

**Questions to ask:**
- Can you return if this doesn't work?
- What opportunities close behind you?
- How hard is it to reverse this decision?
- What's your exit strategy from the exit?
